# New government Reform's

1. Economy of conquest

- [ ] Just like the germans did it, now your overextension penalties are flipped! If you have uncored provinces you are able to continually improve this buff as you continue to conquor land, however you have to be careful to balance the land you do have cored as if you exceed more provinces uncored than cored you will begin to fire the disaster "The war to end all wars" which upon firing will send an event to your rivals to declare war on you at the same time. They won't be able to take land directly but will be able to release states, return cores, take money, pillage, and change religion.

2. Disarmed state

- [ ] This government reform will disable your army, you will not be able to summon mercenaries or deploy and land forces or navy. However you will benefit from having massively increased diplmoatic reputation, more diplo slots, development cost reduction fort maintnence reduction and your vassels will no longer gain desire from independence from army/navy size.

3. Anarchism

- [ ] This government reform doesnt let autonomy go below 90% however it gives massive buffs to manpower, army cost and production (Power to the workers). However you also cannot collect tax at all (taxation is theft). You won't have a ruler anymore and will instead start with "The will of the people", war exhaustion will now fire events that will lower your point generation from your leader as the people continue to get warn out from war. If you dont have any uncored provinces and your war exhaustion is at zero (You will get events gradually) you can eventually get back up to your ruler having 5/5/5 however this can also be buffed further with government reforms to either reduce the war exhaustion penalty or buff your leader past 6/6/6 in peace time based off prosperity.
